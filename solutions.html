Rural Mini-grids & Refugee Settlements

Our Rural Microgrid Development and Operation Model scales reliable power to areas either unserved by conventional utilities or ineligible for grid extension. We construct standalone and hybrid solar photovoltaic and battery-powered plants that operate autonomously from a country's main power supply.

Mini-grids transform lives. Children study at night, existing businesses expand and buy new appliances, and new business ideas come to life. Reliable electricity is a foundational element for communities to achieve economic growth.

  • Autonomous operation independent of main grid
  • Flexibility to interconnect with other mini-grids and/or main grid
  • Hybrid solar, battery, and wind capability
  • Scalable from small villages to large settlements
  • Proven impact on income and education
  • Specialized expertise in refugee contexts

Commercial & Industrial Solar

The market for C&I solar in Africa is growing rapidly as businesses seek ways to cut power costs, supplement overwhelmed grid infrastructure, and reduce their carbon footprint. We're recently built 50 new installations at United Bank of Africa branches across Nigeria, demonstrating our capability to execute large-scale commercial projects.

Our commercial solar solutions help businesses achieve energy independence, reduce operational costs, and meet sustainability goals while supporting Africa's transition to clean energy.

  • Significant reduction in electricity costs
  • Improved energy reliability and independence
  • Reduced carbon footprint and ESG compliance
  • Scalable from small businesses to large corporations
  • Proven track record with major financial institutions

Service Models Available

EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction)

Full construction services where the customer owns the system from day one and pays upfront or arranges their own financing.

  • Customer owns the system
  • Maximum long-term savings
  • Immediate tax benefits
  • Full control over operations

PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)

We finance, build, and operate the system while the customer purchases clean electricity at a predetermined rate, typically lower than grid rates.

  • No upfront capital required
  • Immediate savings on electricity
  • We handle all maintenance
  • Predictable energy costs

Overview

The Distributed Renewable Energy - Agriculture Modalities (DREAM) initiative will build the first solar mini grid powered large scale irrigation systems in Africa, providing farmers with reliable, affordable, and sustainable irrigation. Partners involved in the project include:

  • Ethiopia's Ministry of Water and Energy
  • Ministry of Irrigation and Lowlands (MILLs)
  • Ministry of Agriculture (MoA)
  • Agriculture Transformation Institute (ATI)
  • Global Energy Alliance for People and Planet (GEAPP)
  • The World Bank
  • European Investment Bank and African Development Bank - Sustainable Energy Fund for Africa
